Real-time dynamic fluid surface simulation

Paul Legler, Victor Wang Alex Yang

Problem Description

We want to create a real-time simulation of the surface of a tank of water which reacts interactively to user input. Specifically, we would like to simulate how the water moves when it’s touched with a human finger, taking into account things like touch force and velocity. In addition, we’d like to have the appearance of objects inside the tank update accordingly with the perturbation of the touch.

We think that in addition to modeling the animation of solid objects (the task of project 4), it would be good to study how that differs and the additional challenges that arise from the modeling of fluids. To be able to animate general scenes, it’s important to be able to simulate fluid motion. In terms of applications, real-time fluid simulation can add a crucial level of realism to things like video games and animated movies. There are also forensic applications: a company that Victor worked for over the summer was able to simulate the history of the motion of a part of the ocean in order to determine where an aircraft crashed!

Our problem has a few main components. First is the task of accurately simulating the motion of water. We plan to conduct some research on how fluids move in general; perhaps ask a few TAs for some resources. Next is the task of adding user interactivity: how will we send “touches” to our tank of water? We will probably look up how to add user mouse input and trackpad input to C++ programs. Then, there is the task of how these inputs will manifest as forces in the simulation. This will likely entail more research on how waves emanate from a point on a surface of water, including how they peter out and bounce off of boundaries. Lastly, there is the task of rendering all the water particles.

General Goals:

  • A demo of our project would involve using the trackpad to generate a number of touches and see how the tank reacts
  • A successful project would have reactions in real-time with little delay
  • These reactions would look realistic (how liquids would actually behave)
  • We can have graphs to quantify the accuracy of the particles in the tank
  • Our analysis should answer the following questions:
  • How to accurately model 2D water realistically?
  • How to translate the force of the touch to the wave?
  • How realistic is the model? What noticeable differences are there between this project an actual tank of water?

Deliverable 1 (what we plan to deliver):

  • Model a tank of water with point-mass particles (similar to Project 4)
  • Simulation of motion with external forces, neighboring particles, particle forces
  • In particular react to “touches” -- like dipping your finger into the water
  • Some research needed on physics of water particle motion
  • Shadows from the portion of water molecules in the wave having a higher z coordinate
  • Multiple touches can have waves combine

Deliverable 2 (what we hope to deliver):

  • Add texture to the bottom of the tank
  • Add other types of liquid (with different viscosity)
  • What if someone drags their finger? We can model this as a number of taps or maybe in some other way
